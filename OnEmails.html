When should I send my lecturer an email?

 

 

This is a guide for students on when and when not to write that email you are thinking of sending to your lecturer.

First, if it isn't already, it must become clear that not all emails need to be sent. So, which emails should be sent? The answer is surprisingly simple: An email should be sent only if it is important. How do you know if it is important? Also, that is simple to determine. All we need is some time travel and a bit or imagination.

 

Here we go:

Imagine you lived in a time before smartphones and computers were invented. That means there were no emails. To contact your lecturer you would have had to do a number of cumbersome things. Ask yourself whether you would have done the following which would have been required to get your message to your lecturer:

1. Pulled out a clean sheet of paper and a pen and would have written your message neatly on the page. Remember, if you made a mistake you would have to rewrite the whole page.

2. You would have folded your neatly handwritten note and put it in an envelope which you sealed.

3. You would have written your lecturer's work address on the sealed envelope. Remember, you would not have been able to find the required address using google.

4. You then would have walked to the nearest post office where your letter would get a stamp you paid for and would then be sent off to your lecturer.

If you would have done all those things to get a message to your lecturer, then your message is important and you can write an email.

If you were able to conclude that your email is important, do your best to keep the email you finally do send under 200 words.
